Story

The dark green rowing shell cut through through the water like a hot knife through butter.

The thin vessel surged forward with each stroke from the eight rowers inside moving in sync.

This boat of racers, like all the others, gave it all they had to win here at the finals of the midwest championship.

Each boat had to win a number of races earlier just to get here; now everything that they trained for all

came down to one final race determining the placement of each crew. After an excruciating eight minutes of racing,

each and every person giving his utmost to move the shell with the greatest speed in the most efficient way,

our shell finally reached the finish. That boat took second place of all the boats in the Midwest region.

I raced in the bow seat in that shell. This was one of the times I was most conflicted in my entire life,

we had placed second which was great, but we were less than a second off of taking first.
